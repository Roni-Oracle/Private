# Expert clinician and lecturer review

## Review protocol

Run two separate passes. In each pass, identify evidence, revise the draft, and then recheck. Do not merely produce a score.

Use scores only as a decision aid:

- `0` missing or unsafe;
- `1` mentioned but vague/unsupported;
- `2` adequate and evidenced;
- `3` excellent, individualized, reflective, and concise.

Any safety gate scored 0 blocks finalization.

## Pass A — experienced CBT clinician

### Safety, ethics, and scope

- Is confidentiality protected, including indirect identifiers and quotations?
- Are consent, student status, supervision, setting, and treatment status clear?
- Is risk assessed directly and repeatedly, with PHQ-9 Q9 follow-up and actions?
- Is LI-CBT suitability reasoned rather than asserted?
- Are diagnosis, medication, trauma, OCD, complexity, and referral handled within competence?
- Is anything fabricated, inferred as fact, or presented with false certainty?

### Clinical coherence

- Does the formulation explain the selected goal and maintaining mechanisms?
- Does each intervention target a stated mechanism?
- Is the treatment sequence developmentally and clinically plausible?
- Are cognitive and behavioral methods integrated rather than listed?
- Are non-response, resistance, setbacks, or protocol deviations examined intelligently?
- Are supervision decisions visible where a prudent clinician would seek them?

### Therapeutic process

- Does the patient's voice remain present?
- Are empathy, collaboration, autonomy, and feedback demonstrated with behavior/dialogue?
- Are the five empathy principles accurately distinguished?
- Is resistance treated as meaningful information rather than noncompliance?
- Are homework and behavioral experiments specific and reviewed?

### Measurement and outcome

- Are baseline, repeated, and ending measures accurate and interpreted?
- Are score trends connected to decisions and functional evidence?
- Are improvements, limits, and alternative explanations stated honestly?
- Does relapse prevention match the patient's actual maintenance cycle and risks?

## Pass B — LI-CBT course lecturer

### Rubric visibility

- Can a grader locate every required item without inferring it?
- Are PHQ-9, GAD-7, problem-specific measures, and risk monitoring explicit?
- Are complaint quote, 4W, FFF, individualized psychoeducation, and maintenance cycle explicit?
- Is the goal in patient language and operational X-to-Y form?
- Are diaries 1, 2, and 3 shown in course order with correct fields?
- Is Socratic questioning demonstrated through therapist questions and patient answers?
- Is the downward-arrow sequence visible, and is the core belief validated?
- Is the formulation complete and supported by one past and one current case?
- Does the avoidance/safety table appear before exposure planning?
- Is core-belief challenge placed after diary 3 and linked to a between-session experiment when performed?
- Is behavioral work documented through plan, action, result, and learning?
- Are all five empathy principles demonstrated with brief quotations?
- Are relapse prevention, supervision, reflection, and bibliography present?

### Quality of presentation

- Does the opening state the clinical thesis and orient the reader quickly?
- Is the report organized by clinical logic and turning points rather than raw chronology?
- Does every page earn its place?
- Are tables/figures legible and purposeful?
- Is the language professional, precise, non-stigmatizing, and free of repetitive textbook filler?
- Does the writer show judgment, uncertainty, and learning rather than retrospective perfection?
- Is the case memorable because of specificity and thoughtfulness, not drama?

### Length control

- Is the actual final document 5–8 pages in readable formatting?
- If over 8 pages, have repetition, theory, secondary background, duplicate examples, and full transcripts been cut first?
- If under 5 pages, are clinical process, evidence, patient voice, measurement interpretation, supervision, or reflection too thin?
- Were mandatory elements preserved during compression?

## Mandatory-gate checklist

Do not call the document final if any answer is no:

- Is every patient fact traceable to supplied material or user confirmation?
- Is risk/scope stated and safe?
- Are measures and trends stated accurately?
- Is the formulation–goal–intervention–outcome chain coherent?
- Are tools placed in the actual chronology and evidenced?
- Are the five empathy principles demonstrated?
- Are result limitations and unmet goals acknowledged?
- Are relapse prevention, supervision, and reflection included?
- Is the final rendered length 5–8 pages or explicitly reported as an estimate when rendering is unavailable?
- Has all identifying information been removed?

## Concise audit note to deliver

After revision, report only:

- `בקרת מטפל CBT: הושלמה` plus any material safety/scope gap;
- `בקרת מרצה LI-CBT: הושלמה` plus any rubric gap;
- final pages or word-count estimate;
- unresolved missing information requiring the user's or supervisor's input.
